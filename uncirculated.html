Uncirculated Fake Internet Money

Fake Internet Money didn't happen over night. It was a roughly two month process of experimentation and iteration. I started with a concept and a few ideas, but I didn't have a clear vision of how the full series would fit together until the final weeks of the project.

As the project took form I occasionally took snapshots of bills that stood out to me. In some ways these snapshots log the progress of the project, as features and components came, went, progressed, and regressed. But these are not representative samples of the state of the algorithm when they were taken. In many cases they were the first time I saw a specific pattern or combination, and shaped the direction I ended up taking.

As I continued to add features and movetowards completion I began paying more and more attention to consistency. This unfortunately meant that I had to cut things that I liked but didn't quite fit. Additionally, I estimate that I looked through around 8,000 iterations between my first draft and the final product. As a result, I came across many fantastic iterations that were not fated to make it into circulation.

So I'm left with a collection of images that are part sketchbook, part historical log, and part longing at what could have been.
